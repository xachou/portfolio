My Work

To further immerse myself in the edging human computer interaction practice, I visited the Haptic Lab of Purdue University. I am the first undergraduate student ever visit- researched in this lab and I am the only student who majored in design. It’s challenging to collaborate with the developers who haven’t trained by design thinking before. To solve the poor experience of VR baseball training programs, I utilized my experiences in 3D modeling and swiftly learned the use of MAYA( a specific model development software for VR environment). I hence created a baseball stadium and install it into the VR environment to make the training experience more plausible. I learned that the design works are constrained by the technology margin and eager to access more disparate technologies. So I can understand what’s the effectiveness of the technology and how to transfer the design into practical solution efficiently.
